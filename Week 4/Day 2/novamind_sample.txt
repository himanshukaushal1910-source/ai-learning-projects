NovaMind Internal Update — Q2 Review and Q3 Planning
=====================================================
Date: July 1, 2024
Author: Sarah Chen, Chief of Staff
Distribution: All Hands

---

PRICING MODEL DECISION — Q2 OUTCOME

After extensive review during Q2, the leadership team has finalised NovaMind's pricing
structure moving forward. Following three months of customer interviews, competitive
analysis and internal modelling, we have decided to adopt a per-seat subscription model
effective August 1, 2024. The new pricing tiers are as follows: Starter at $12 per seat
per month for teams of up to 10, Growth at $9 per seat per month for teams of 11 to 50,
and Enterprise at $7 per seat per month for teams exceeding 50 seats. The flat-rate model
that was previously under consideration was ultimately rejected after feedback from our
enterprise pilot customers indicated that it created friction during procurement. Per-seat
pricing aligns NovaMind's revenue directly with customer team size and perceived value.
The sales team will begin outreach to existing beta customers this week to migrate them
to the new structure. All contracts signed before August 1 will be honoured at their
original terms through December 2024.

---

ENGINEERING ROADMAP — Q3 PRIORITIES

The engineering team has finalised the Q3 roadmap following last week's planning session.
The primary focus for July through September will be API performance and reliability.
Benchmark testing conducted in June revealed that our core project retrieval endpoint has
a p95 latency of 840 milliseconds under moderate load, which is significantly above our
internal target of 300 milliseconds. Reducing this latency is the team's top priority for
Q3. The approach will involve database query optimisation, introduction of a Redis caching
layer for frequently accessed project data, and a migration of our search infrastructure
to an indexed vector-based approach. Secondary priorities include completing the mobile
application redesign that was carried over from Q2, and shipping the Slack integration
that has been in beta with three pilot customers since May. The Slack integration is
currently on track for general release in late August. All feature work beyond these
priorities has been pushed to Q4 to allow the team to focus on stability and performance.

---

HIRING UPDATE — NEW ROLES AND TIMELINE

NovaMind is expanding the team in Q3 with three new hires across two departments. The
engineering team is hiring two senior backend engineers to support the performance
initiatives outlined above. Both roles are currently live on our careers page and have
been shared with our recruiting partners. We have received 47 applications to date and
first-round interviews begin next Monday. Target start date for both engineering roles
is September 1. Additionally, the customer success team is hiring one Customer Success
Manager to support our growing enterprise customer base following the pricing transition.
This role will focus on onboarding, retention and expansion revenue. The CSM position
is being handled directly by the Head of Customer Success and is expected to be filled
by mid-August. All three hires have been approved in the current operating budget and
do not require additional board sign-off. Team leads will be notified once offers are
extended so they can prepare onboarding plans accordingly.
